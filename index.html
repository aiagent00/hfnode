Why Antarctica Matters

Antarctica is Earth’s natural climate regulator, reflecting sunlight, storing freshwater, and hosting unique species found nowhere else.

Our Mission in the Frozen South

Antarctic Shield is a collaborative initiative focused on protecting Antarctica’s fragile ecosystems through science-based conservation, international cooperation, and public awareness.

From melting ice shelves to vulnerable penguin colonies and krill populations, the changes in Antarctica are a warning signal for the entire planet.

We work with researchers, policy makers, and communities worldwide to reduce human impact, expand marine protected areas, and promote responsible tourism.

  • Monitor ice loss and sea-level rise trends
  • Protect marine life, including krill, seals, whales, and penguins
  • Promote low-impact research and eco-tourism practices
  • Support strong international environmental agreements

Key Conservation Initiatives

We focus on concrete, science-based actions to protect Antarctica’s ice, wildlife, and surrounding Southern Ocean.

Ice & Climate Monitoring

Supporting long-term monitoring of ice sheets, glaciers, and sea ice to better understand and respond to global sea-level rise.

Learn More

Wildlife Protection

Protecting penguin colonies, seals, krill, and whales by advocating for sustainable fisheries and minimizing disturbance.

Learn More

Southern Ocean Protection

Promoting large-scale marine protected areas to safeguard key feeding grounds and migration routes in the Southern Ocean.
